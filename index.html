Deep Thoughts

The Parallel Between Circuits and Society

Just as electrical circuits have resistance, capacitance, and inductance that shape current flow, human societies have cultural, economic, and political elements that shape the flow of ideas and progress.

Can we engineer better societies using principles from electrical engineering? Maybe feedback loops in circuits hold lessons for social systems.

Education as a Transformer

Like a transformer changes voltage levels, education transforms human potential. My experience in both Saudi and UK education systems showed me how different approaches can step up or step down a student's capabilities.

What would an ideal educational "transformer" look like? One that efficiently matches input potential to output requirements without losses.

The Game of Life

Developing games made me realize life itself follows game mechanics - quests (goals), XP (experience), NPCs (people who don't affect your journey), and bosses (major challenges). But unlike video games, we can't reload after failure. This makes risk calculation crucial in real life's RPG.
